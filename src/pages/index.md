Carlos Pizzino received his PhD and MSc degrees in Electrical Engineering, with a specialisation in Control, Automation and Robotics, from the Federal University of Rio de Janeiro (COPPE/UFRJ) in 2024 and 2008, respectively. He also holds a BSc degree in Electronic Engineering from the Federal Centre of Technological Education of Rio de Janeiro (CEFET/RJ), awarded in 2006.

From 2020 to 2022, he served as a Research Engineer with the Brazilian Air Force at the Institute of Airspace Control. Prior to that, from 2009 to 2020, he worked as an Electronics Engineer at the Brazilian Air Forceâ€™s Department of Airspace Control. In 2020, he was a Visiting Researcher at the Robotics Laboratory within the School of Mathematical and Computer Sciences at Heriot-Watt University, Edinburgh, United Kingdom. He is currently a Robotics Researcher at Ingeniarius, Porto, Portugal.

His research interests include robot localisation and mapping based on neuroscience-inspired models, as well as sensor fusion, SLAM, localisation, autonomous navigation, and decision-making for autonomous robotic systems.
